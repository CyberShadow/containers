std.allocator.Region
struct Region(uint minAlign = platformAlignment)
A allocator manages one block of memory provided at construction. There is no deallocation, and once the region is full, allocation requests return . Therefore, s are often used in conjunction with freelists, a fallback general-purpose allocator, or both.
The region stores three words corresponding to the start of the store, the current position in the store, and the end of the store. One allocation entails rounding up the allocation size for alignment purposes, bumping the current pointer, and comparing it against the limit. The parameter establishes alignment. If , the sizes of all allocation requests are rounded up to a multiple of . Applications aiming at maximum speed may want to choose and control alignment externally.
